Airport Security Procedures

At the airport, you’ll undergo several security checks. Here's a brief overview of what you can expect:

  • Identification and Boarding Pass: Always have your government-issued ID and boarding pass ready.
  • Baggage Screening: Your carry-on and checked baggage will be screened for prohibited items using advanced X-ray technology.
  • Personal Screening: You'll pass through metal detectors or body scanners. Be prepared for additional screening if necessary.
  • Security Personnel: Our highly trained security staff ensures compliance with all regulations for your safety and security.
Pro Tip: To make the security process smoother, wear easily removable shoes, avoid large metal accessories, and pack your liquids in containers no larger than 100ml in a clear, resealable bag.

Prohibited Items: Pack Smart, Travel Safe

It's important to pack only approved items in your carry-on and checked baggage. Certain items are strictly prohibited for safety reasons:

  • Firearms and Weapons: Guns, ammunition, knives, and sharp objects are not allowed in carry-on baggage.
  • Flammable Items: Lighter fluid, propane, and other hazardous materials are restricted.
  • Liquids Over 100ml: You can carry small quantities of liquids, gels, and aerosols, but they must be in containers of 100ml or less, placed in a transparent bag.
  • Explosives: Fireworks, flares, and other explosive items are prohibited in both carry-on and checked luggage.
Important: Always review the list of prohibited items before packing. If you're unsure, contact our support team or check our Prohibited Items page for the latest information.

Know Your Safety Protocols: In-Flight Procedures

At Sunshine Airlines, we want you to be well-prepared in the unlikely event of an emergency. Our flight crew is trained to handle a wide range of scenarios, and here’s what you should know:

  • Seatbelts: Keep your seatbelt fastened while seated. This simple action can prevent injury during unexpected turbulence.
  • Oxygen Masks: In the event of cabin pressure loss, oxygen masks will drop down. Secure your own mask before assisting others.
  • Life Vests: Under your seat, you'll find a life vest. In case of emergency, follow the crew’s instructions for proper use.
  • Emergency Exits: Take note of the nearest exit in relation to your seat. Our crew will guide you in case of evacuation.
Stay Calm: In any emergency, remaining calm and following the crew’s instructions will ensure your safety. Our staff is trained to handle any situation professionally.

Pro Tips for a Safe Journey

Here are some expert tips to make your travel experience as smooth and safe as possible:

  • Keep Your Seatbelt Fastened: Even when the seatbelt sign is off, keeping your seatbelt fastened can prevent injury in unexpected turbulence.
  • Familiarize Yourself with Safety Instructions: Read the safety card in the seat pocket. It contains crucial information about emergency exits and procedures.
  • Know Your Exits: Count how many rows away the nearest exit is from your seat. In an emergency, knowing this could save your life.
  • Report Any Medical Issues: If you feel unwell, inform a flight attendant immediately. They are trained to provide assistance and can access first aid supplies.
